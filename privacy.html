Short version: Clinical Simulation collects minimal data. We do not store personal health information. The platform may display ads to support free access. This page explains exactly what we collect and why.
Section 01

Overview

Clinical Simulation ("the Platform", "we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy describes how we handle information when you access or use our platform at clinic.rajatcj.com (or any associated subdomain).

Clinical Simulation is a free, solo-developed educational platform. We operate with a minimal data footprint by design — we do not require user accounts, do not collect names or email addresses during normal use, and do not monetise user data in any way.

This policy applies to all users of the Platform regardless of location.

Section 02

Data We Collect

We collect only the data necessary to operate, improve, and secure the Platform. This falls into two categories:

Automatically collected data

Data type Examples Purpose
Usage analytics Pages visited, case started/completed, time on page Understand how the Platform is used; improve case design
Device & browser info Browser type, operating system, screen resolution Ensure compatibility; diagnose rendering issues

Data you provide voluntarily

If you contact us via email (e.g., to report a case error), we will receive your email address and any information you include in your message. This data is used solely to respond to your query and is not added to any mailing list or shared with third parties.

We do not collect:

  • Personal health information or medical history
  • Payment information (the Platform is free)
  • Precise GPS location data
  • Sensitive personal data as defined under GDPR or equivalent regulations
Section 03

How Data Is Used

Any data we collect is used exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Platform operation: Ensuring the Platform functions correctly across devices and browsers.
  • Educational improvement: Understanding which cases are most used, where learners struggle, and how to improve clinical content.
  • Security and abuse prevention: Detecting and blocking unusual or abusive access patterns.
  • Aggregate reporting: Generating anonymised, aggregate statistics about Platform usage (e.g., "X cases completed this month"). These reports do not identify individuals.

We do not use your data for profiling, automated decision-making, or any commercial purpose beyond serving contextual advertisements through Google AdSense.

Section 05

Cookies & Local Storage

Clinical Simulation uses local browser storage (localStorage / sessionStorage) and cookies to improve performance and reduce unnecessary server calls. Specifically:

  • Session caching: User profile data, scores, and case progress may be cached locally to avoid repeated Firestore reads and speed up page loads.
  • Authentication state: Firebase sets cookies and localStorage entries to persist your login session across page reloads.
  • Preferences: UI preferences and settings are saved locally on your device.

This locally stored data:

  • Is never shared with advertising networks or third parties beyond what Firebase requires for authentication
  • Can be cleared at any time through your browser settings — note that clearing storage will log you out and reset any locally cached progress

We do not use tracking cookies or advertising cookies. Firebase may set its own cookies as part of authentication; these are governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

Section 06

Data Retention

We retain data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy:

  • Analytics data is retained in aggregate, anonymised form. Individual-level analytics (if any) are retained for no longer than 12 months.
  • Server access logs are retained for a short operational period (typically 30–90 days) and then discarded.
  • Your local progress data is stored only in your own browser and is under your full control.
  • Account data (email, display name, scores) is retained until you delete your account. Upon deletion, all associated Firestore data is permanently removed within 30 days.

You may request deletion of any data we hold about you at any time by contacting us at the address below.

Section 07

Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights regarding your personal data. Regardless of where you are located, we will honour the following requests:

  • Access: Request a summary of any personal data we hold about you.
  • Deletion: Request that any personal data we hold about you be deleted.
  • Correction: Request correction of any inaccurate personal data we hold.
  • Objection: Object to how we are processing your data.
  • Portability: Receive a copy of your data in a portable format.

To exercise any of these rights, including accessing, correcting, or deleting your account data, contact us using the details in Section 11.

We will respond to all valid requests within 30 days.

Section 08

Minors

Clinical Simulation is intended for users aged 16 and above, as stated in our Terms of Service. We do not knowingly collect personal data from individuals under the age of 16.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe that a child under 16 has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately and we will take steps to delete that information.

The Platform does not contain content targeted at children, and we do not use any data practices specifically directed at minors.

Section 09

Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect any data we hold from unauthorised access, loss, or disclosure. These include:

  • HTTPS encryption for all Platform traffic
  • Minimal data collection by design, reducing exposure risk
  • Access to any stored data restricted to the developer only

However, no system is perfectly secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, and we encourage you to avoid sharing sensitive personal information over any web platform.

In the unlikely event of a data breach affecting personal information, we will notify affected users and relevant authorities as required by applicable law.
