Our Mission

Why this exists

Medical education in Nepal and across South Asia has long relied on memorisation over reasoning. Students can recite Davidson's by chapter, but freeze at the bedside when presentations overlap, budgets are constrained, and every decision has real consequences.

MedSim Nepal was built to bridge that gap — a simulation engine that places learners inside the Emergency Department, the Medicine Ward, the OT — and forces them to think, not just recall. Every case is written to MBBS-level clinical standards, grounded in Davidson's, Harrison's, Bailey & Love, and WHO guidelines, and set in the hospitals and communities of Nepal.

This is not a quiz app. It is a clinical reasoning trainer.

Technology

How it's built

MedSim Nepal is intentionally minimal in its technology stack — fast to load, no build step, deployable anywhere. Cases are authored as structured JSON files that the engine interprets at runtime. The simulation logic, progression system, and scoring rubric are all driven by data, not hardcoded behaviour.

Vanilla HTML5 CSS3 (no frameworks) Vanilla JavaScript JSON Case Engine IBM Plex Mono Newsreader DM Sans
Medical Accuracy Disclaimer
All clinical content is authored for educational simulation purposes and is based on Davidson's Principles of Medicine (23rd Ed.), Harrison's Principles of Internal Medicine (21st Ed.), Bailey & Love's Short Practice of Surgery (27th Ed.), and applicable WHO guidelines. This platform is not a substitute for clinical training, clinical judgment, or professional medical advice. Cases are designed for MBBS-level learners under the supervision of qualified faculty.
